The Current Situation: UV Radiation and Atmospheric Heating

The Sun emits a broad spectrum of electromagnetic radiation, including UV rays (UV-C, UV-B, UV-A). The Earth's atmosphere, particularly the stratospheric ozone layer, absorbs most harmful UV radiation.

UV Irradiance is Rising ‚òÄÔ∏è

Research indicates that UV irradiance at the surface is rising in many places due to factors like ozone depletion, changes in cloud/aerosol cover, and climate-driven shifts. If more UV and solar radiation reach the lower atmosphere/surface, the surface/air can warm more, potentially changing the structure of the troposphere.

The Solution: Marine Cloud Brightening (MCB) üí°

Marine Cloud Brightening (MCB) is a geoengineering technique aiming to **increase the reflectivity (albedo)** of low-lying clouds to reflect more solar radiation back into space, moderating global warming.

**MCB Deployment Concept:** A visualization of a specialized ship releasing **aerosolized saline solution** to brighten clouds, enabling the $\text{MCB}$ process.

MCB Process Flow

**1. Seed Clouds:** Clouds are seeded with tiny sea-salt particles acting as **Cloud Condensation Nuclei (CCN)**.
**2. Smaller Droplets:** This creates **more, smaller droplets**, increasing cloud albedo (the Twomey effect).
**3. Increased Reflection:** More solar energy (including UV) is reflected back to space.
**4. Cooling Effect:** Less solar energy absorbed by Earth's surface leads to a **net cooling effect**.

By adjusting cloud reflectivity, we can modulate the Earth‚Äôs radiative balance, reducing net energy gain in the climate system.
